
I think falling in love with someone while you already hate yourself, is one of the most confusing things your mind can go thought. Because the feeling grows inside you, while another voice inside your head keeps reminding you that you're probably the last person they would ever choose. You watch them talk, you watch the way they exist so normally in the world. And then you look at yourself, and the comparison happens automatically without even trying, and its brutal. Because it makes you painfully aware of every single thing you already hate about yourself, the way you look, the way you act, the way you exist in a room, and suddenly liking them starts feeling almost embarrassing. Like you caught yourself wanting something you were never supposed to want in the first place. And the worst part is, you dont even wait for them to reject you, because your brain already did that for them long before they ever had the chance. You start convincing yourself that they deserve someone better, someone who isint constantly fighting their own reflection. Someone who doesn't feel like a mistake standing next to them, and it messes with you, because the feeling for them doesn't go away, you still crave their attention, you still want their messages. You still imagine what it would feel like if they actually chose you, but at the same time, there's this heavy truth sitting in your chest. That keeps whispering that people like them, dont end up with people like you. And its not even jealousy or anger, its just this quite sad acceptance, that loving them was always going to stay something that only existed inside your own head. Because when you hate yourself this deeply, it becomes almost impossible to believe that the person you want the most, could ever look at you and genuinely think your worth choosing over anyone else.
